"Be Right Back" Episode of Black Mirror

  • By Courtney Sheridan

At the beginning of the semester, we watched the Black Mirror episode titled “Be Right Back”. This episode is about a woman who takes advantage of a service that uses artificial intelligence to create a voice and text - and then physical - replica of her late boyfriend. I’d seen this episode once before we watched it in class, and both times I was left feeling pretty uncomfortable. The music and tone of the episode alongside the contents of the episode (the AI-recreated version of a person who had passed away) contributed to my feelings about it.

This episode introduced us to one of the main discussions we would have in this class: What kind of influence can technology have on our world, and do we want it to have that influence? While the technology presented in this episode of Black Mirror feels futuristic and like a work of fiction, it isn’t hard to argue that we’re much closer to that possibility than we’d like to think. The technology that allows the woman to call and text her late partner is artificial technology that we could build today. The AI takes in the data of all of the man’s texts, social media posts, and phone calls and uses those to predict what he might have said in new situations. Barring the ethical concerns of the AI having access to all of that data, this technology is all readily available in the real world. So, do we want it to be?

“Make Love, not Warcraft" Episode of South Park

  • By Courtney Sheridan

In the “Make Love, not Warcraft” episode of the popular animated television show “South Park”, some of the characters go on a journey in the game World of Warcraft to level up in order to defeat a more powerful character. In their comedic efforts, they become essentially glued to their screens, barely moving to eat or use the restroom. This satirical commentary on MMORPG players addresses stereotypes of those players. In addition to the main kids playing the game, they highlight the way in which other adults interact with the game: the powerful character is a guy similarly glued to the chair with many snacks scattered on the table, one kid’s dad loves the game too but feels like he can’t participate with his kid, and the Blizzard management characters don’t play the game themselves. The game also demonstrates the idea of “grinding” to get more experience points since the kids spend all of their time in the game killing one type of creature. This episode makes fun of how people interact with World of Warcraft in the real world and causes us to examine the stereotypes associated with the game.
